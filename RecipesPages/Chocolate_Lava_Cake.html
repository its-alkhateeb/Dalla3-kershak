Chocolate Lava Cake

A decadent, melt-in-your-mouth chocolate cake with a warm molten center, served with a cold scoop of vanilla ice cream.

⏱ Prep Time: 10 Mins
🔥 Cook Time: 12 Mins
🍽 Servings: 2 People
🥗 Category: Dessert / International

Description

The Chocolate Lava Cake, also known as Moelleux au Chocolat, is the ultimate indulgence for chocolate lovers. It features a lightly baked outer shell that conceals a rich, liquid chocolate core. When served warm with a cold ball of vanilla ice cream on top, it creates a perfect balance of temperatures and textures that is truly irresistible.

Ingredients

  • 100g High-quality dark chocolate
  • 1/2 cup Unsalted butter
  • 2 large Eggs + 2 Egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup Granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp All-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp Vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 scoops Vanilla ice cream

Instructions

  1. Melt: Melt the dark chocolate and butter together in a heat-proof bowl over simmering water (or in the microwave) until smooth.
  2. Whisk: In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, yolks, sugar, and vanilla until thick and pale. Fold in the melted chocolate.
  3. Combine: Gently sift the flour and salt into the mixture. Fold until just combined—do not overmix.
  4. Bake: Pour the batter into greased and floured ramekins. Bake at 200°C for 10-12 minutes until the edges are firm but the center is soft.
  5. The Flip: Let the cakes cool for 1 minute, then carefully run a knife around the edge and invert them onto a plate.
  6. The Topping: Immediately place a large scoop of cold vanilla ice cream on top of the hot cake and serve instantly.

Technical Notes

Timing is Key: Every oven is different. If you bake it for 1 minute too long, the center will solidify and you lose the "lava" effect. Pro-Tip: Dust the ramekins with cocoa powder instead of flour to avoid white streaks on your dark chocolate cake.
