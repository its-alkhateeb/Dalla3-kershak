Crispy Beef Sambosa

A quintessential crispy appetizer: golden fried pastry pockets filled with savory spiced meat.

⏱ Prep Time: 30 Mins
🔥 Cook Time: 10 Mins
🍽 Servings: 24 Pieces
🥗 Category: Appetizer / Egyptian

Description

Sambosa is a beloved savory pastry enjoyed throughout the Middle East. In Egypt, these thin, triangular pockets are typically filled with a mixture of minced meat, onions, and warm spices, then deep-fried to a perfect golden crunch. They are the ultimate "finger food" and a staple at every Egyptian gathering.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack Sambosa wrappers
  • 500g Ground beef
  • 1 large Onion (finely diced)
  • 1/2 cup Fresh parsley
  • 1 tsp Allspice (Baharat)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 tbsp Flour + 2 tbsp Water
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. The Filling: Sauté diced onions until soft, add ground beef and cook until browned, then stir in spices and parsley. Let cool.
  2. The Glue: Mix the flour and water into a thick paste to use for sealing the pastry edges.
  3. Folding: Place a teaspoon of filling at the end of a pastry strip and fold diagonally to form a triangle.
  4. Zig-Zag: Continue folding the triangle along the strip in a zig-zag motion until you reach the end.
  5. Sealing: Dab a small amount of the flour paste on the remaining edge and tuck it into the fold to seal tightly.
  6. Frying: Heat oil in a deep pan and fry sambosa in batches for 2-3 minutes until golden brown and bubbly.

Technical Notes

Storage: Best served fresh, but can be frozen uncooked for up to 3 months in airtight bags. Pro-Tip: Keep wrappers under a damp cloth while working to prevent them from drying and cracking.
