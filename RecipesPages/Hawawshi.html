Egyptian Hawawshi

A street-food favorite: spiced minced meat baked inside crispy, oven-toasted Baladi bread.

⏱ Prep Time: 20 Mins
🔥 Cook Time: 25 Mins
🍽 Servings: 4 People
🥗 Category: Appetizer / Egyptian

Description

Hawawshi is a traditional Egyptian meat pita that is both juicy on the inside and crunchy on the outside. It consists of a seasoned ground beef mixture—flavored with onions, bell peppers, and a signature spice blend—stuffed into fresh pita bread. The pockets are then brushed with oil or butter and baked until the bread becomes golden and crisp. It is typically served hot with tahini dip and spicy pickled vegetables (Torshi).

Ingredients

  • 4-5 Fresh Baladi pita breads
  • 500g Ground beef (20% fat)
  • 1 large Onion (grated and drained)
  • 1 Green bell pepper (finely chopped)
  • 1 Chili pepper (optional)
  • 1 tsp Hawawshi Spice (Cumin, Coriander, Allspice)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Butter or Vegetable oil (for brushing)

Instructions

  1. The Meat Mix: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, grated onion, chopped peppers, and all the spices. Knead the mixture by hand until well combined.
  2. The Bread: Using a knife or your hands, carefully open one edge of the pita bread to create a pocket. Do not cut it all the way through.
  3. Stuffing: Take a handful of the meat mixture and spread it into a thin, even layer inside the bread. Flatten it out so it cooks evenly.
  4. Coating: Lightly brush the outside of the bread on both sides with oil or melted butter. This is the secret to the perfect crunch.
  5. Baking: Wrap each loaf in foil (optional for softer bread) or place directly on a baking tray. Bake at 200°C for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  6. Serving: Cut the rounds into halves or quarters and serve immediately while the bread is still hot and crispy.

Technical Notes

The Fat Content: For the best flavor, use ground beef with a higher fat percentage. The fat melts into the bread during baking, giving it that iconic street-food taste. Pro-Tip: If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can cook the Hawawshi on a charcoal grill or in a heavy cast-iron skillet on the stovetop.
